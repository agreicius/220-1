Example 5.9. Classic trig limits.

Compute \(\lim\limits_{\theta\to 0}\frac{\sin(2\theta)}{\theta}\text{.}\)
Solution.
We give two computations. The first uses the double-angle formula.
\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{\theta\to 0}\frac{\sin(2\theta)}{\theta} \amp = \frac{2\sin\theta\cos\theta}{\theta} \amp \text{(double-angle)} \\ \amp =2\lim_{0\to \theta}\frac{\sin\theta}{\theta}\lim_{\theta\to 0}\cos\theta \amp \text{(scal. mult., prod.)}\\ \amp = 2\cdot 1 \cdot 1 \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_trig_lims.html}{\text{5.8}}, \text{cos eval.})\text{.} \end{align*}
The second uses a substitution technique that I call affine substitution. See RemarkÂ 5.10 for a discussion of its legitimacy.
\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{\theta\to 0}\frac{\sin(2\theta)}{\theta}\amp = \lim\limits_{\theta\to 0}\frac{2\sin(2\theta)}{2\theta}\\ \amp = 2\lim\limits_{t\to 0}\frac{\sin(t)}{t} \amp (t=2\theta)\\ \amp = 2 \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_trig_lims.html}{\text{5.8}})\text{.} \end{align*}
