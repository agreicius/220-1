Theorem 21.3. Mean value theorem (MVT).

Assume \(f\) is continuous on \([a,b]\) and differentiable on \((a,b)\text{.}\) There is an input \(c\in (a,b)\) satisfying
\begin{equation} f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\text{.}\tag{21.1} \end{equation}

Proof.

We employ a little trick that reveals MVT to be a simple consequence of Rolle’s theorem. Define
\begin{equation*} g(x)=f(x)-\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{(b-a)}(x-a)\text{.} \end{equation*}
As is easily verified, \(g\) is continuous on \([a,b]\text{,}\) differentiable on \((a,b)\) and satisfies
\begin{align} g(a) \amp =g(b)=f(a)\tag{21.2}\\ g'(x) \amp = f'(x)-\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{(b-a)}\text{.}\tag{21.3} \end{align}
Applying Rolle’s theorem to \(g\) on \([a,b]\text{,}\) we conlude that
\begin{equation*} g'(c)=0 \end{equation*}
for some \(c\in (a,b)\text{.}\) We conclude, using (21.3) we conclude that
\begin{equation*} 0=f'(c)-\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\text{,} \end{equation*}
or equivalently
\begin{equation*} f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\text{,} \end{equation*}
as desired.
