Theorem 6.10. Continuous functions.

  1. Absolute value.
    The function \(f(x)=\abs{x}\) is continuous everywhere on its domain. In other words, the absolute value function is a continuous function.
  2. Polynomials and rational functions.
    If \(f\) is either a polynomial or a rational function, then \(f\) is continuous everywhere on its domain. In other words, polynomials and rational functions are continuous functions.
  3. Trigonometric functions.
    The functions \(f(x)=\sin x\text{,}\) \(g(x)=\cos x\text{,}\) and \(h(x)=\tan x\) are continuous everywhere on their domains. In other words, \(\sin\text{,}\) \(\cos\text{,}\) and \(\tan\) are continuous functions.

Proof.

  1. This is a direct consequence of Definition 6.2 and Theorem 3.11.
  2. This is a direct consequence of Definition 6.2 and Corollary 2.17.
  3. This result actually requires some work, since currently we only have an evaluation limit formula for sine at \(0\text{.}\) This gives us a chance to illustrate the affine substitution technique again. (See Remark 5.10.) Take any \(a\in \R\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{align*} \lim_{x\to a}\sin x\amp = \lim_{x\to a}\sin ((x-a)+a) \\ \amp = \lim_{x\to a}(\sin(x-a)\cos a+\cos(x-a)\sin a) \amp \text{(trig. ident.)}\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to a}\sin(x-a)\lim_{x\to a}\cos a+\lim_{x\to a}\cos(x-a)\lim_{x\to a}\sin a \amp \text{(sum, prod. rule.)}\\ \amp = \lim_{x\to a}\sin(x-a)\cos a+\lim_{x\to a}\cos(x-a)\sin a \amp \text{(const. rule)}\\ \amp = (\lim_{t\to 0}\sin(t))\cos a+(\lim_{t\to 0})\cos(t)\sin a \amp (\text{subst. } t=x-a)\\ \amp =0\cos a+1\sin a \amp (\knowl{./knowl/xref/th_lim_sine_cos.html}{\text{3.12}})\\ \amp = \sin a\text{.} \end{align*}
    The computation shows both that (i) the limit of \(\sin\) exists at \(a\text{,}\) and (ii) the limit agrees with the value of \(\sin\) at \(a\text{.}\) We conclude that \(\sin\) is continuous at \(a\text{.}\) Since \(a\) was arbitrary, we conclude that \(f\) is continuous everywhere, hence continuous.
