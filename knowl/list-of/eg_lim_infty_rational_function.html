Example 8.11. Limit at infinity: rational function.

Let \(f(x)=\displaystyle\frac{3x^3-100x}{4\abs{x}^3+x^2+1}\text{.}\) Compute \(\lim\limits_{x\to \infty}f(x)\) and \(\lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}f(x)\text{.}\)
Solution.
Note that both the numerator and denominator approach \(\pm \infty\) as \(x\to \pm \infty\text{.}\) Our intuition is that the highest power of \(x\) is the “dominant” term for large \(x\text{.}\) To crystallize this intuition algebraically, we factor out the \(x^3\) in the top and bottom, after first dealing with the absolute value:
\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{x\to \infty} \frac{3x^3-100x}{4\abs{x}^3+x^2+1}\amp = \lim\limits_{x\to \infty}\frac{3x^3-100x}{4x^3+x^2+1} \amp (x\geq 0\implies \abs{x}=x) \\ \amp = \lim\limits_{x\to \infty}\frac{x^3(3-\frac{100}{x^2})}{x^3(4+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x^3})}\\ \amp = \lim\limits_{x\to \infty}\frac{3-\frac{100}{x^2}}{4+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x^3}} \amp (\text{repl.}) \\ \amp\frac{3-100\lim\limits_{x\to\infty }\frac{1}{x^2}}{4+\lim\limits_{x\to\infty }\frac{1}{x}+\lim\limits_{x\to\infty }\frac{1}{x^3}} \amp (\text{quot., sum, scal. mult.}) \\ \amp = \frac{4+0}{3+0+0} \amp (1/x^n\to 0)\\ \amp = \frac{4}{3}\\ \lim\limits_{x\to -\infty} \frac{3x^3-100x}{4\abs{x}^3+x^2+1}\amp = \lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}\frac{3x^3-100x}{-4x^3+x^2+1} \amp (x\leq 0\implies \abs{x}=-x) \\ \amp = \lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}\frac{x^3(3-\frac{100}{x^2})}{x^3(-4+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x^3})}\\ \amp = \lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}\frac{3-\frac{100}{x^2}}{-4+\frac{1}{x}+\frac{1}{x^3}} \amp (\text{repl.}) \\ \amp\frac{3-100\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty }\frac{1}{x^2}}{-4+\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty }\frac{1}{x}+\lim\limits_{x\to-\infty }\frac{1}{x^3}} \amp (\text{quot., sum, scal. mult.}) \\ \amp = \frac{4+0}{-3+0+0} \amp (1/x^n\to 0)\\ \amp = -\frac{4}{3}\text{.} \end{align*}
