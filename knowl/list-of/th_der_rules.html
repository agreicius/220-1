Theorem 12.4. Derivative rules.

  1. Linear combination.
    For all constants \(c,d\in \R\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation} \frac{d}{dx}(cf(x)+dg(x))=cf'(x)+dg'(x)\text{.}\tag{12.4} \end{equation}
  2. Product rule.
    We have
    \begin{equation} \frac{d}{dx}(f(x)g(x))=f'(x)g(x)+f(x)g'(x)\text{.}\tag{12.5} \end{equation}
  3. Quotient rule.
    We have
    \begin{equation} \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right)=\frac{f'(x)g(x)-f(x)g'(x)}{\left(g(x)\right)^2}\text{.}\tag{12.6} \end{equation}
