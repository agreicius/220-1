Example 9.9. Infinite limit: polynomial.

Compute \(\lim_{x\to -\infty}x^3+7x^2\text{.}\) Your answer should be a real number or \(\pm \infty\text{.}\)
Solution.
Note first that \(\lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}x^3=-\infty\) (9.6), and \(\lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}7x^2=\infty\) (type \(\infty\cdot c\)). Unfortunately, we do not have any infinite limit principles with descriptive type \(\infty-\infty\text{,}\) so we cannot use any of the results of Theorem 9.7 directly. Instead, we first do some algebra, using our intuition that the \(x^3\) term “dominates” the \(7x^2\) term:
\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}x^3+7x^2 \amp = \lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}x^3(1+7/x) \\ \amp = -\infty \amp (\text{type } \infty\cdot c)\text{,} \end{align*}
since \(\lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}x^3=-\infty\) and \(\lim\limits_{x\to -\infty}1+\frac{7}{x}=1+0=1\text{.}\)
