Example 13.2. Height versus age.

Suppose the average height \(h\) (in meters) of an individual in a population is given as a function \(h=f(a)\) of their age \(a\) (in years).
  1. Interpret the two statements below in terms of the height and age of people. Your interpretation should include units, and should address all numeric details in the statements.
    \begin{align*} f(12)\amp = 1.5\\ f'(12) \amp = 0.06 \text{.} \end{align*}
  2. What does the following sequence of statements tell us about the relation of height versus age? Be specific.
    \begin{align*} f'(12) \amp = 0.06\\ f'(15) \amp = 0.025\\ f'(18) \amp = 0.001\text{.} \end{align*}
