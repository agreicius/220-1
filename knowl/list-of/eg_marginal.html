Example 13.6. Marginals.

Suppose the cost \(C\) (in dollars) for a farmer to produce \(q\) barrels of milk on a given day is given by
\begin{equation*} C=f(q)=100+10q-\frac{q^2}{100}+\frac{b^3}{250}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Suppose further that farmer can charge a price \(P\) of
\begin{equation*} P=\frac{20000}{100+b} \text{ dollar per barrel} \end{equation*}
when producing \(b\) barrels of milk.
  1. Determine the marginal cost of producing 100 barrels of milk.
  2. Determine the marginal profit of producing 100 barrels of milk.
  3. Estimate the profit of of producing 103 barrels of milk.
